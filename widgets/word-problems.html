Word problems

Word problems are translation exercises: the hard part isn't the arithmetic, it's turning a sentence into the right calculation. Read for what's actually asked, keep the numbers that matter, and let the wording point you to the operation.

the numbers → the operation → the answer

Let the wording choose the operation. "per", "each", or "% of" means multiply; a coupon or discount means subtract; "how much to fill / finish" asks for the missing part (target − current); and a rate that changes partway means split into pieces and add. Use the numbers that matter, and ignore any thrown in to distract.
