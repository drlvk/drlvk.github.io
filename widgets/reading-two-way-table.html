Reading a two-way table

A two-way table sorts people by two characteristics at once. Reading it well means knowing where to look — a single cell, a row or column total, or the grand total — and, for a percent, which total belongs in the denominator. Every question below refers to this one table.

percent = part ÷ the right total × 100

Each cell counts people in one row and one column; the margins are the row and column totals, and 250 is the grand total. A "percent of all" divides by 250; "among the East office" divides by the East total (200), not by 250; and "Sales or West" adds the Sales and West totals, then subtracts the one cell counted in both. Multiply the resulting fraction by 100 to write it as a percent.
