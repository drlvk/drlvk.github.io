Multi-step word problems

A multi-step problem is built from several smaller pieces. Work out each piece on its own — usually a rate times an amount — then combine them, adding what's gained and subtracting what's lost.

total = (rate × amount) + (rate × amount) + …

Break the problem into pieces and compute each as rate × amount, then combine them. Gains and bonuses add; losses, penalties, and returns subtract — so a rate can be negative. Don't forget a fixed base that's charged no matter what, and ignore any piece that contributes zero except to keep your tally honest.

rate — value per item (+ or −) amount — how many of that item total — all the pieces combined
