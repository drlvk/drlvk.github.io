Counting with overlapping groups

When two groups overlap, you can't just add them — anyone in both gets counted twice. Subtract the overlap once, and everything else (the total in at least one group, the "neither", the "one only") follows.

A or B = A + B − both

The overlap is counted twice — once in A, once in B — so subtract both once to get how many are in at least one group. Everything else follows: neither = total − (A or B); A only = A − both; and you can rearrange to find the overlap itself: both = A + B − (A or B).
