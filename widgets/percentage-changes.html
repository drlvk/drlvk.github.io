Percentage changes

Every percentage change is really a multiplier. Once you see it that way, stacked changes are just multipliers in a row — and they combine in ways that don't always match your guess.

new = old × (1 ± rate)

A rise of 25% multiplies the price by 1.25; a drop of 15% multiplies by 0.85. To find a percent change, divide the change by the starting value: (new − old) ÷ old. When changes stack, multiply the factors one after another — which is why two steps rarely add up to what you'd expect.

old — the starting value rate — the change, as a decimal new — the result
