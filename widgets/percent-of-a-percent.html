A percent of a percent

When a percentage is taken of a group that is itself only a percentage of the whole, the two don't add — they multiply. A percent of a percent is always a smaller share of everyone.

share of whole = group % × within %

Turn both percents into decimals and multiply: a 25% slice of a 60% group is 0.60 × 0.25 = 0.15, i.e. 15% of everyone. For a head count, multiply by the whole as well. And when a population splits into two groups, find each group's piece separately, then add them.

group % — the slice of everyone within % — the slice of that group share — the overlap, out of everyone
