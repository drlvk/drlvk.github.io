Given a sequence \(\{x_n\}\) in \(\ell^2\) with \(\|x_n - x_{n+1}\| \le 2^{-n}\text{,}\) we:

  1. Show that it converges coordinate-wise to something (call it \(x\)), meaning \(x_n(k)\to x(k)\) for \(k\in \mathbb N\text{.}\)
  2. Construct a dominating function (sequence) \(z = |x_1| + \sum_{n=1}^\infty |x_n-x_{n+1}|\text{.}\) Here \(z(k)\) is finite and \(|x_n(k)|\le |z(k)|\) for every \(n, k\text{.}\) Consequently \(|x(k)|\le |z(k)|\text{.}\)
  3. Prove that \(z\in \ell^2\text{:}\) this follows from the Monotone Convergence Theorem and the fact that the \(\ell^2\) norm of any partial sums in the definition of \(z\) is bounded by \(\|x_1\|_2 + \sum_{n=1}^\infty 2^{-n} = \|x_1\|_2 + 1\text{.}\)
  4. Apply the Dominated Convergence Theorem: since \(|x_n(k)-x(k)|^2 \to 0\) for each coordinate \(k\text{,}\) and \(|x_n(k)-x(k)|^2\le 4|z(k)|^2\text{,}\) it follows that \(\sum_k |x_n(k)-x(k)|^2 \to 0\text{.}\) This means \(\|x_n-x\|\to 0\) as required.
