Let \(L\) be bounded and not identically zero. Let \(\Mi = \ker L\text{,}\) a proper linear subspace. Pick a nonzero vector \(h\in\Mi^\perp\text{.}\) Consider the functional \(f(x)=\dfrac{L(h)}{\|h\|^2}\inn{x,h}\text{.}\) It agrees with \(L\) on \(\Mi\) and at \(h\text{.}\) But every element of \(\Hi\) admits orthogonal decomposition \(x = x' + th\) where \(x'\in \Mi\) and \(t\in \C\text{.}\) Hence \(L(x) = tL(h) = tf(h) = f(x)\) for every \(x\in \Hi\text{.}\)
