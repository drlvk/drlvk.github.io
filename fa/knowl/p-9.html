For finite-dimensional vector spaces, the standard inner product is \(\sum_{k=1}^n x(k)\overline{y(k)}\text{.}\) More generally, if there is a measure \(\mu\) on the space \(\Omega\text{,}\) we can do \(\int_\Omega x\overline{y}\,d\mu\text{.}\) Special cases: \(\Omega=[0, 1]\) with the Lebesgue measure; \(\Omega=\mathbb N\) with counting measure.
