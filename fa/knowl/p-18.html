Let's prove that \(\ell^2\) is complete. The plan is:

  1. Given a Cauchy sequence \(\{x_n\}\text{,}\) prove that it converges coordinate-wise to something (call it \(x\)), meaning \(x_n(k)\to x(k)\) for \(k\in \mathbb N\text{.}\)
  2. Prove that the coordinate-wise limit \(x\) is also in \(\ell^2\text{.}\)
  3. Prove that \(x_n\to x\) in the \(\ell^2\) norm.
