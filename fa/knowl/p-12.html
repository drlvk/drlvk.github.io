In general, a norm is homogeneous, positive except at \(0\text{,}\) and satisfies the triangle inequality. An inner product induces a norm by \(\|x\|=\sqrt{\inn{x,x}}\text{.}\) The triangle inequality is not obvious, and for it we need the CBS inequalityin its abstract form \(\abs{\inn{x,y}}\le \|x\| \|y\|\text{.}\) Originally it was proved for sums, then for integrals. Often attributed to Cauchy-Schwarz, omitting Bunyakowsky.
