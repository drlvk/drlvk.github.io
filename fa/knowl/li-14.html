Item 2.
The space \(S\) of simple functions (those that attain finitely many values) is dense in \(L^2([a, b])\text{.}\) Idea of proof: suppose \(f\in S^\perp \setminus \{0\}\) and use the Lebesgue differentiation theorem to find a simple function \(g\) (the characteristic function \(\chi_I\) of some interval \(I\)) such that \(\inn{f, g}\ne 0\text{.}\)
