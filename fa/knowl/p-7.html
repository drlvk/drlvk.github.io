As a matter of fact, we could talk about functions \(f:\Omega\to\mathbb C\) where \(\Omega\) is a completely abstract set; then of course there is no continuity or differentiability. If \(\Omega\) has \(n\) elements, this is an \(n\)-dimensional space, if infinite; it's infinite-dimensional. Sequences are just functions with \(\Omega=\mathbb N\text{.}\) It is often more convenient to retain the functional notation, writing elements of sequence \(x\) as \(x(k)\) instead of \(x_k\text{.}\)
