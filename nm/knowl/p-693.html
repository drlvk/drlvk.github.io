A cubic spline is a function \(S\) that is piecewise a degree 3 polynomial, and such that \(S, S', S''\) are continuous (have the same value from both sides at the points where pieces meet). In our context of fitting to data points \((x_k, y_k)\text{,}\) \(k=1, \dots, n\text{,}\) it is natural to have each cubic polynomial defined on \([x_{k}, x_{k+1}]\text{.}\) The continuity of first and second derivatives means that the graph of a cubic spline has no corners of sudden changes of curvature, it flows quite naturally.
