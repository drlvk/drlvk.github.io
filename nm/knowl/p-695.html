Formally, piecewise linear interpolation means that given some points \(a=x_1 \lt x_2 \lt \cdots \lt x_n = b\) on an interval \([a, b]\) and corresponding y-values \(y_1, \dots, y_n\text{,}\) we define a function \(S\colon [a, b]\to \mathbb R\) by the piecewise formula

\begin{equation} S(x) = y_k + \frac{y_{k+1}-y_k}{x_{k+1} - x_{k}} (x - x_k),\quad x_{k}\le x\le x_{k+1}\tag{23.1.1} \end{equation}

where the right hand side is a linear function through two points \((x_{k}, y_k)\) and \((x_{k+1}, y_{k+1})\text{.}\) So, instead of \(1\) interpolating polynomial of degree \(n-1\) we use \(n-1\) polynomials of degree \(1\text{.}\) The function \(S\) is a spline of degree 1 meaning it is a piecewise function where the pieces are polynomials of degree 1.
