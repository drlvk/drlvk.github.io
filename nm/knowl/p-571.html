Monte-Carlo method can also be used to average a function over a sphere with the help of normal distribution. Namely, the commands

x = randn(1, d);
x = x/norm(x);

generate a \(d\)-dimensional random vector of unit length, and such vectors are uniformly distributed over the unit sphere in \(\mathbb R^d\text{.}\) This works specifically for the normal distribution because of the following property of its probability density function \(p\text{:}\) the product \(p(x_1)p(x_2)\cdots p(x_d)\) depends only on the norm of vector \(\mathbf x\text{.}\) Using this random vector in a loop, like in ExampleÂ 18.3.1, we find an approximate value of the average of \(f\) over the sphere \(\{\mathbf x\in \mathbb R^d\colon |\mathbf x|=1\}\text{.}\)
