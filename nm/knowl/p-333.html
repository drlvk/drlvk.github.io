For notational convenience, we express a nonlinear system of \(n\) equations with \(n\) unknowns as a vector equation \(\vec F(\vec x)=0\) where \(\vec x\) is an unknown vector with \(n\) components. For example, to express (10.1.1) in vector form we write

\begin{equation} \vec x = \begin{pmatrix} x \\ y \end{pmatrix},\quad \vec F(\vec x) = \begin{pmatrix} x_1^2e^{3x_2}-30 \\ x_1 x_2 - \sin(x_1 + x_2^2) \end{pmatrix}\tag{10.1.2} \end{equation}
