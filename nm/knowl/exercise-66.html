A power law is a function of the form \(y = a x^p\text{.}\) By taking logarithm on both sides we get \(\log y = p \log x + \log a\text{,}\) which is a linear function of \(\log x\text{.}\) So, to fit a power law to given data \((x_k, y_k)\text{,}\) we apply the logarithm to both \(x_k\) and \(y_k\text{,}\) and follow the linear regression process.
