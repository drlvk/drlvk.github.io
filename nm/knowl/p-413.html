Recall from ExampleÂ 12.3.1 that the symmetric difference formula has error of order 2. Therefore, in the equation (12.4.3) we have \(2\text{.}\) The approximation \(A_1\) is the original formula

\begin{equation*} A_1 = \frac{f(x+h)-f(x-h)}{2h} \end{equation*}

To get \(A_2\) we replace \(h\) by \(2h\text{:}\)

\begin{equation*} A_2 = \frac{f(x+2h)-f(x-2h)}{4h} \end{equation*}

The formula (12.4.3) now tells us that

\begin{equation} f'(x) \approx \frac{4 A_1 - A_2}{3} = 2\frac{f(x+h)-f(x-h)}{3h} - \frac{f(x+2h)-f(x-2h)}{12h}\tag{12.4.4} \end{equation}

which is the extrapolated approximation to \(f'(x)\text{.}\)
