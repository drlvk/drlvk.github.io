(Theoretical problem.) Suppose that Newton's method is used to solve \(f(x)=0\) with \(f(x) = e^x + e^{-x} - 2\text{.}\) Show that the function \(g(x)=x-f(x)/f'(x)\) (extended by \(g(0)=0\)) has a nonzero derivative at \(0\) by computing the limit of \(g(x)/x\) as \(x\to 0\text{.}\)
