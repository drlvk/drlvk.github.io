Most importantly, the minimization problem (28.1.2) can be easily solved using the method of ChapterÂ 27. For notational convenience let \(\beta_1= \log a\) and \(\beta_2 = b\text{.}\) If the left hand side of (28.1.2) was zero, that would mean that \(\beta_1, \beta_2\) is a solution of the overdetermined linear system

\begin{equation} \beta_1 + \beta_2 x_k = \log y_k,\quad k=1, \dots, n\tag{28.1.3} \end{equation}

The least squares solution of (28.1.3) is the vector \(\beta = (\beta_1, \beta_2)\) that minimizes (28.1.2).
