This section takes another look at the logistic function (29.1.2) in preparation for the homework assignment. The meaning of parameter \(A\) is not very intuitive, which motivates the following equivalent form of the function:

\begin{equation} y = \frac{M}{1 + e^{-r(x-a)}}\tag{29.4.1} \end{equation}

(so, \(A = e^{ra}\text{.}\)) Note that this curve is obtained from the standard logistic function

\begin{equation*} \sigma(x) = \frac{1}{1+e^{-x}} \end{equation*}

with

  • vertical stretching by factor \(M\text{;}\)
  • shift to the right by \(a\) units;
  • horizontal compression by factor \(r\text{.}\)
