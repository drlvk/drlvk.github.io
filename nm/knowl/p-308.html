Once we have the function \(g\text{,}\) the rest of the process is the same as in Chapter 8, in particular in Example 8.2.1. Changing the first two lines of that program to

g = @(x) 2*x^3/(3*x^2-1); 
x0 = 0.6;

we get “Found x = 1 after 11 steps”. If the starting point is different, the process may converge to a different root of the equation \(x^3-x = 0\text{.}\) For example with x0 = 0.57 we get “Found x = -1 after 14 steps”. A small change of initial condition produced a rather different solution; we went from finding x = 1 to finding x = -1. The pattern of what starting points produce which solution can be very complicated: see the illustrations on Wikipedia page Newton fractal.
