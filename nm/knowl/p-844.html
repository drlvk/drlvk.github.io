If we can find the derivative \(f'\) (or gradient \(\nabla f\) in higher dimensions), it may be possible to apply one of root-finding methods of PartÂ II to it. For example, Newton's method was one of those. But since we are solving \(f'=0\text{,}\) the method will involve the second derivative of \(f\text{:}\) it amounts to iteration of the function

\begin{equation} g(x) = x - \frac{f'(x)}{f''(x)}\tag{30.3.1} \end{equation}
