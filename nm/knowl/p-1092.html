We look for a line that minimizes the sum of penalties, the penalty being the amount by which a point falls into the wrong group. Formally, we minimize \(\sum p_j\) where \(p_j\ge 0\) are the penalties, subject to constraints \(y_j\ge ax_j + b - p_j\) for points in the “top” category and \(y_j \le ax_j + b + p_j\) for points in the “bottom” category. This is still a linear programming problem, but there are more variables now: \(a, b, p_1, \dots, p_n\text{,}\) and the objective function is \(\sum p_j\text{.}\)
