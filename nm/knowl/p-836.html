Since the standard logistic function rises from 0 to 1 and has maximal slope when \(x=0\text{,}\) we can relate parameters \(a, M, r\) directly to shape of the curve:

  • \(a\) is the x-value at which the curve has the steepest slope;
  • \(M\) is the amount by which the curve rises;
  • \(r\) measures its steepness; the curve is essentially flat outside of the interval \([a-5/r, a+5/r]\text{.}\)
