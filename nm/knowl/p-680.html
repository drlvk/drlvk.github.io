The knots of a spline are the transition points where it turns from one polynomial to another. These are often, but not always, the points \(x_k\) from the given data. Requiring \(S'''\) to be continuous at \(x_2\) implies we have the same polynomial on both intervals \([x_1, x_2]\) and \([x_2, x_3]\) (why?), and therefore \(x_2\) is not a knot for this spline. Same for \(x_{n-1}\text{.}\) The not-a-knot condition is the standard choice when we do not have any reason to impose other conditions.
