A simple way to arrive at Simpson's rule is to observe that Midpoint rule and Trapezoidal rule have errors of opposite signs due to concavity. This suggests averaging them to improve the accuracy. But the simple average \((M+T)/2\) would not achieve much because the error of midpoint rule is about half of the error of trapezoidal rule, according to the estimates in ChapterÂ 13. Instead, the weighted average \((2M+T)/3\) is used, giving weight \(2/3\) to Midpoint and \(1/3\) to Trapezoidal. The result is Simpson's rule.
